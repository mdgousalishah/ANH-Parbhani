About Us

Our History, Our Mission, and Our Enduring Legacy

A Legacy from the Nizam Era

The Masjid e Mehtab Ali Shah complex is a cherished landmark in Parbhani, established as a service endowment (Khidmat-e-Mash) in October 1880, according to the historic deed Muntaqab No. 25624.

Officially recognized as a Waqf property in the Maharashtra Government Gazette on May 2, 1974, the complex has grown into a multifaceted institution. Today, it not only serves the spiritual needs of our community but also faces the challenge of preserving its unique heritage against illegal encroachment, a testament to its historical and ongoing significance.

Our Endowed Land

The total endowed land for the complex is 13 acres and 23 gunthas, spread across three survey numbers:

  • Survey No. 1: 3 acres, 36 gunthas
  • Survey No. 2: 8 acres, 16 gunthas
  • Survey No. 3: 1 acre, 11 gunthas

From this, 4 acres from Survey No. 2 were leased to the Parbhani Municipal Council in 1959. Unfortunately, much of the remaining land is currently illegally occupied, and efforts are underway to reclaim and protect this sacred endowment for community use.

Our Leadership

A sacred trust passed down through generations.

The First Mutawalli: Mahemood Ali Shah Roshan Ali Shah

As the first Mutawalli, Mahemood Ali Shah Roshan Ali Shah was the original caretaker of this sacred endowment. He laid the foundation for the spiritual and community life of the complex, establishing a legacy of service and devotion. His leadership was instrumental in shaping the institution into the cornerstone it is today.

Our Current Leadership

The administration of the complex is overseen by the Mutawalli and Sajjada Nashin, a hereditary role appointed by the Maharashtra State Waqf Board. The Mutawalli is responsible for preserving the heritage of the complex and ensuring it serves the community's needs.

Mohammed Mushtaque Ahmed, Mutawalli

We are honored to have Sajjada Nashin Va Mutawally Mohammad Mushtaque Ahmed Mohammad Ali Shah as our current Mutawalli. He is actively working with the Waqf Board and government officials to address the significant issue of illegal encroachment on the Masjid's land. His dedicated efforts are focused on protecting this sacred property for future generations.

Our Mission & Vision

Our mission is to serve as a vibrant center for Islamic life, dedicated to spiritual growth, education, and community service. We strive to preserve our rich heritage while fostering an inclusive environment where all can gather, learn, and worship in peace and harmony.
