Mutawalli Meets with Waqf Board on Encroachment

Mutawalli Mushtaque Shah met with Waqf Board President Mr. Mirza to discuss the illegal encroachment on the Masjid's land and praised the board for issuing notices to the encroachers. The meeting was a crucial step in the ongoing efforts to reclaim and protect the sacred property for community use.

Demand to Remove Encroachment on Survey No. 1-2-3

A formal request has been submitted to the Minority Affairs Minister, Abdul Sattar, to take legal action against illegal occupants on the registered Waqf land of Ashoorkhana Nale Hyder and Masjid e Mehtab Ali Shah. The request highlights the long-standing issue and urges the government to intervene.

Municipal Officials Inspect Waqf Board Land

Following a notice from the Waqf Board, municipal officials conducted a site inspection of the land leased to the municipality, as the 30-year lease has expired. The inspection is the first step in the process of reclaiming the land for the Waqf institution.

610 Individuals Receive Notices from Waqf Board

In a second phase of action, the Waqf Board has issued notices to 610 occupants on the Masjid's land, warning of criminal proceedings if they do not provide a satisfactory explanation within four weeks. This move has been widely welcomed by the community.

Minister Orders Report on Waqf Property Encroachment

The Minister for Minority Development, Dattatray Bharane, has ordered an immediate inquiry and report on the complaints of illegal encroachment and dealings on Waqf properties in Parbhani and other areas. The report is expected to be submitted within a month.
